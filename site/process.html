How We Work

No slide decks.
No vague sprints
that deliver nothing.

Our process is built around one principle: you should see a working system as early as possible — not a prototype, not a wireframe. A working system.

01
Week 1 – 2

Discover & Map

Before we suggest anything, we understand your system. Where decisions get made. How information flows. Where the actual bottlenecks are — not the ones you think are the problem, but the ones that show up when you map the real workflow.

We're looking for specific leverage points where technology creates compounding value, not marginal improvement.

Workflow mapping Bottleneck ID Tech stack audit Scoping doc
02
Week 2 – 4

Prototype & Validate

We build fast, working prototypes using the right stack for your specific problem. You see a real, functional system early — not a slide deck with mockups.

This phase is honest. If something doesn't work as expected, we tell you immediately and adjust. No sunk cost thinking — if the prototype reveals a better approach, we take it.

Working prototype Architecture decision Stack validation Benchmarks
03
Week 4 – 8

Deploy & Refine

Production deployment, integration with your existing tools and infrastructure, and team enablement. We don't disappear after launch — we stay close through the first real-world usage cycles, when the unexpected things always happen.

The goal is your team's independence, not their dependency on Navonmesh for every change.

Production deploy Integration testing Team handoff Documentation Post-launch support
Honest disclaimer

What we don't do.

✕
Vague strategy reports

We don't produce 40-page PDFs with frameworks and no action items. If you want something to file away, we're not the right fit.

✕
Open-ended retainers

We don't do open-ended engagements with no clear output. Every project has a defined scope, deliverable, and end date.

✕
Untested tool recommendations

We won't recommend a stack we haven't personally built with. If we haven't hit the failure modes, we're not qualified to send you in blind.

✕
Quantity over quality

We take on a limited number of projects at a time. More clients means less attention per client — and that's not a trade-off we'll make.
